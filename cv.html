Current Work

 

See below to learn about some of my current projects. You can download a copy of my CV here.

 

Associations between fine particulate matter, extreme heat events, and congenital heart defects

I am working with environmental epidemiologist Dr. Jeanette Stingone for my master’s research in Columbia’s Department of Epidemiology.

We are assessing the effects of maternal exposure to air pollution (PM2.5) and extreme heat events on congenital heart defects in offspring. Our research is part of the National Birth Defects Prevention Study (NBDPS), one of the largest studies of its kind in the US.

 

Climate variation and under-5 acute malnutrition in Bangladesh

I am also working with Columbia’s International Research Institute for Climate and Society (IRI), a part of the Earth Institute.

Specifically, I am working with Dr. Hannah Nissan and Dr. Robbie Parks to assess associations between climate events and child anthropometry at seasonal and sub-seasonal scales, with a current focus on Bangladesh.

My research is funded by ACToday, Columbia University’s first World Project.
